## Design Context

### Users
Nostalgic retro gamers who love classic arcade aesthetics and throwback experiences. They play for the love of vintage gaming culture, neon-soaked visuals, and the underground arcade scene. They appreciate authentic retro vibes over polished corporate design.

### Brand Personality
Gritty. Underground. Raw.
- Emotional goal: Nostalgia mixed with edge—like a darkened arcade at 2am
- Voice: Direct, unfiltered, anti-corporate

### Aesthetic Direction
**Gritty Synthwave (Dark Synth / Outrun Grime)**
- References: John Carpenter movie posters, underground 80s rave flyers, distressed neon signage, CRT monitor glow
- Anti-references: Polished glassmorphism, purple-to-blue AI gradients, rounded cards, generic "gaming" sites
- Theme: Dark mode default with light mode toggle (light mode should feel like a blown-out CRT in daylight)
- Colors: Deep dark with oklch-neon pinks (#ff2d55), electric cyan (#00f0ff), warning orange—applied with texture and noise, never flat
- Typography: Bebas Neue (display - tall, condensed, rave-poster energy) + Saira (body - angular, sharp, unpolished feeling)

### Design Principles
1. **Grit over gloss** — No glassmorphism, no smooth gradients, no rounded-everything. Surfaces should feel textured, imperfect, alive
2. **Neon with restraint** — Synthwave palette used intentionally with grime: flickering effects, scanlines, distressed grid lines
3. **Asymmetric attitude** — Break the grid, vary spacing dramatically, left-align text, create visual tension
4. **Raw functionality** — No decorative sparkles or meaningless animations. Motion conveys state changes with exponential easing
5. **Underground edge** — This isn't a friendly corporate dashboard. It's a dimly-lit arcade. Typography is tight, spacing is intentional, vibe is uncompromising
